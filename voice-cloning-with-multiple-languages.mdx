You can use Sendspark voice cloning in almost any language — just speak that language and keep saying the English placeholder word "Watermelon" where a name should go. Watermelon is the keyword that tells the system to swap in each contact's name. To fix names that come out Americanized, import the phonetic spelling as the First Name.

Learn how to produce different pronunciations throughout many languages in our AI Voice Cloning feature.

## Key takeaways

- The word **WATERMELON** is the keyword that tells the system to switch to the contact names you added.
- It only works **once**, within the first ~10 seconds of the video, and should follow a greeting (e.g. "Hola Watermelon," "Hallo Watermelon").
- You can clone your voice in any language by speaking it — just keep the placeholder word "Watermelon" in English.
- If names come out Americanized, import the phonetic pronunciation and use it as the "First Name" field; ChatGPT can generate these at scale.

## Let's Set Ground Rules for Voice Cloning:

- The word **WATERMELON** serves as a keyword for the system to recognize and switch over to the names you added.
- It only works **ONCE** during the initial seconds of your video. If you say "Watermelon" after the first 10 seconds of the video, we'll assume you are talking about the fruit 🍉
- It should be accompanied by a **greeting**: "Hey Watermelon," "Hallo Watermelon," "Hola Watermelon," etc.

## Can I Clone My Voice in Languages Other Than English?

You should be able to use voice cloning with any language, simply by speaking that language. You will just need to keep using the placeholder word "Watermelon" in English.

For example, these should all work:

`​​Spanish: "Hola Watermelon, aquí hay un vídeo para ti..."`

`​​German: "Hola Watermelon, Hier ist ein Video für Sie..."​​French: "Bonjour Watermelon, voici une vidéo pour vous..."​`

---

## How Do I Specify the Dialect?

We've had some customers report that Sendspark "Americanizes" their accents when saying customer names. For example, even though you're speaking German, Sendspark might still use the American pronunciation of certain names.

One workaround that you can use right now is, when you import your contacts, specify the phonetic pronunciation of the names.

If you have a large list, you can ask ChatGPT to do this for you at scale.

**`Prompt:`**` Please provide the phonetic pronunciations for these names written out, as if you were a native [Language] speaking from [Region]. Please format the response in a list, without the original names, bullet points or any formatting: [List of Names].`

**For example:**

```text
Please provide the phonetic pronunciations for these names written out, as if you were a native German speaking from Berlin. Please format the response in a list, without the original names, bullet points or any formatting: SophiaLeonHannahPaulMiaTheoEllaLucaMilaFinnLinaLiamLiaEmilLeniLouisClaraHenryLeaBen
```

ChatGPT will then provide the phonetic pronunciation for you, and you can use that as the "First Name" field in Sendspark to ensure that your voice cloning comes out the way you'd like:
