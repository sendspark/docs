The "created with Sendspark" text appears on a LinkedIn thumbnail because the landing page header was left empty during dynamic campaign setup. Whatever you type in that header shows on the thumbnail, so add text like "Hey First Name" to personalize it. To remove the sendspark.com line, connect a custom domain in your workspace settings.

## Key takeaways

- Whatever you put in the landing page header appears on the LinkedIn thumbnail; an empty header is what produces the "created with Sendspark" legend.
- Add personalization such as "Hey First Name" to the header so the lead's name shows in the thumbnail.
- Connect a custom domain in your workspace settings to remove the sendspark.com line at the bottom of the thumbnail.
- LinkedIn caches thumbnail images, so regenerate the video for a new link if you've already shared it and need the updated thumbnail to appear.

This article will show you how to remove the "created by Sendspark" legend from a thumbnail when sending videos via LinkedIn messages.

When you send a video to LinkedIn messages, you simply paste the video link in the message and send it out. The moment you do that, LinkedIn will automatically deploy a thumbnail.

If you see "created with Sendspark and Sendspark.com" at the bottom of the thumbnail, it's because you haven't fully white labeled your account yet:

![LinkedIn message thumbnail displaying Created with Sendspark branding text](/images/13310440/e83511d9-image.png)

The "Created with Sendspark" legend can be easily removed when you are on the landing page step of generating videos in a dynamic campaign. If created with Sendspark, it is because the header in the landing page section was left empty.

Basically anything that you put in the header will actually come up in that thumbnail.

This is a great opportunity for adding the "Hey First Name" personalization so you can actually have the lead's name in that thumbnail. To do that, see the following image.

![Landing page header field in dynamic campaign video creation interface](/images/13310440/db7601f1-image.png)

IMPORTANT: If you have already shared the video on LinkedIn, notice that they cache images. That means that you'll probably need to regenerate the video so that a new link is provided to avoid LinkedIn pulling a cached thumbnail

Finally, in order to change the sensepark.com at the bottom of the thumbnail, you simply need to connect a custom domain to Sendspark.

Simply head to your workspace settings to start the process:

[https://sendspark.com/settings/workspace](https://sendspark.com/settings/workspace)

![Workspace settings panel with custom domain configuration options](/images/13310440/0e4f22ef-image.png)

We have a dedicated help article that covers the topic:

[Use a custom domain for videos](/use-a-custom-domain-for-videos)

That's all!
