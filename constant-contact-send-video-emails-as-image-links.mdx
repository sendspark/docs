To send a video email in Constant Contact, use an image preview that links to your full video. In Sendspark, download your video's thumbnail (GIF) and copy the share link. Then in Constant Contact, drag in an image block, replace the default image with your Sendspark thumbnail, and link that image to your Sendspark share link so recipients click to watch.

## Key takeaways

- The most reliable way to share video in email is an image preview: recipients see it in their inbox and click "play" to watch the full video in their browser.

One of the most reliable ways of sharing videos in email is to use an image preview. Recipients can see the preview in their inbox, and then click "play" to watch the full video in their browser.

Sendspark thumbnails create dynamic, engaging emails that not only capture attention but also maintain good deliverability, provided they are optimized correctly.

You can easily send Sendspark videos in Constant Contact by following these steps:

- Record or upload your video in [Sendspark](https://sendspark.com/my-videos)
- Click **Share**
- Download the **Thumbnail (GIF)** of your video

![Download Video to Send In Constant Contact](/images/5568771/e925299e-image.png)

- Copy the **Share Link** to your clipboard

![Copy Video Share Link for Constant Contact](/images/5568771/94ab7f90-image.png)

- Navigate to your **email draft** in Constant Contact
- Drag an **image block** into your email

![Drag Video block into Constant Contact](/images/5568771/8dd565e6-image.png)

- **Replace** the default image with your **Sendspark** **thumbnail (gif)**

![Upload Video Preview to Constant Contact](/images/5568771/873e0d9d-image.png)

- **Link** the image to your **Sendspark** **share link**

![Add Video Share Link to Constant Contact](/images/5568771/de42a869-image.png)

- That's it! You can now send this video email 🥳

## Best Practices

- **A/B Testing**: Experiment with plain text vs. video thumbnails to assess their impact on delivery rates and engagement. Evidence suggests plain text emails can improve initial delivery metrics.
- **Campaign Type Optimization**: For cold campaigns, prioritize simplicity and deliverability over aesthetic enhancements like thumbnails.
- **Deliverability Considerations**: Use video thumbnails only when their added value outweighs potential email delivery issues.
