Sendspark lets you record one video and personalize it with each recipient's name at scale in multiple languages. Record your intro using the language-specific placeholder word (for example, "wassermelone" in German or "Manzana" in Spanish), add the video to a Dynamic Video campaign, and select the matching language. Sendspark includes guides for German, Spanish, Portuguese, Swedish, French, Dutch, and English.

## Key takeaways

- Record one video and personalize it with each recipient's name at scale, in your language.
- Sendspark includes step-by-step guides for German, Spanish, Portuguese, Swedish, French, Dutch, and English.
- Record your intro with the language's placeholder word (e.g. "Hallo wassermelone," "Hola Manzana") and select that language in the campaign.
- No guide for your language? Use the English framework, and let Sendspark know if the results aren't good so they can add support.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Which languages does Sendspark provide guides for?">
    Sendspark includes guides for German, Spanish, Portuguese, Swedish, French, Dutch, and English. For any other language you can use the English framework.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What placeholder word do I use in my intro?">
    Use the language-specific placeholder word. For example: "Hallo wassermelone" (German), "Hola Manzana" (Spanish), "Olá melancia" (Portuguese), "Hej vattenmelon" (Swedish), "Salut pastèque" (French), "Hoi Watermeloen" (Dutch), or "Hey Watermelon" (English).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if there's no guide for my language?">
    Try the English framework and see if the outcome is good. If it's not, let Sendspark know and they can add specific support for your language.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does the personalization actually work?">
    You record one video that includes the placeholder word, add it to a Sendspark Dynamic Video campaign, and select your language. Sendspark then replaces the placeholder with each recipient's name at scale.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
