To embed a video that plays right in email, add Sendspark's HTML snippet to your email's HTML and make sure recipients use an email client that supports video, such as Apple Mail, Superhuman, or Outlook for Mac. Because clients like Gmail don't support native video, always include a GIF fallback that opens the video in a browser.

## Key takeaways

- A video plays in-email only when it is embedded in your email's HTML and viewed on a client that supports video, such as Apple Mail, Superhuman, or Outlook for Mac.
- Gmail and Outlook for Windows do not support native video, so it's important to provide a GIF fallback that opens the video in a browser.
- Videos cannot autoplay with sound because email clients block it, so an animated GIF cover is used to capture attention instead.
- To embed, create your video, then copy the email embed code snippet from the Share > Embed tab and paste it into an HTML block in your email platform.

## Will Embedded Videos Always Play In Email?

Even if you embed a video ***perfectly*** in email, it still might not play in email depending on the email client it is being viewed on.

To play in email, a video must (1) be embedded in the HTML of your email (we'll cover how to do this), and (2) must be viewed on an email client that supports video.

Apple Mail, Superhuman, and Outlook for Mac support video in email, but many popular email clients, like Gmail and Outlook for Windows do not support native video. ([See all email clients that support video](https://blog.sendspark.com/which-email-clients-support-embedded-videos)).

For this reason, it's important to provide a "fallback" option for recipients who receive your video, but cannot view it natively in their email client. We will also cover how to do this here.

If your recipients are unable to see the video embed working as intended, troubleshoot by ensuring the email snippet and URL parameters are correctly matched to prevent viewing issues.

## Can Video Emails Autoplay with Sound?

No, you cannot make your videos autoplay with sound.

Email clients block this — for a good reason! Imagine you are checking your email from the library, a public location, or a meeting you are supposed to be paying attention in, and all of the sudden a video starts playing out loud. This would be embarrassing!

That being said, you can get the desired effect of standing out & capturing attention by using an animated GIF as the cover for your video in email.

This way, your viewer opens the email, sees something moving (exciting!), and can click to watch the full video.

Once you are able to edit the HTML, **simply paste the snippet copied to your clipboard into your email.**

## Troubleshooting Common Issues with Personalized Video Embedding

## Problem: Recipients are not seeing the personalized video despite using the email snippet and parameter.

This issue might occur due to:

- Incorrect or mismatched URL and email parameters.
- Improper linking of the personalized video to your campaign.
- Missing or incorrect usage of the video snippet.

## Resolution:

1. Double-check the URL and email parameter to ensure they are correctly matched.
2. Verify that the video is properly linked in your campaign.
3. Ensure the snippet is correctly configured for when the recipient’s email is unavailable.

Following these steps can help ensure videos are displayed as intended, creating a smooth viewing experience for recipients.
