To send a video in a HubSpot email as a clickable image, record or upload your video in Sendspark, download the video thumbnail, add it to an image block in a new HubSpot email, then copy the video URL from Sendspark and link it to that thumbnail. Recipients see a consistent preview image in every email client and click through to your video landing page.

## Key takeaways

- Sending a video as an image + link gives you better tracking (clicks and plays), a consistent preview across Gmail, Outlook, and other clients, and a call-to-action on the landing page to drive conversions.
- The tradeoff versus embedding the video in-line is engagement versus ability to track: linking adds slight friction but captures more data.
- The setup is: record/upload in Sendspark → download the thumbnail → add it to a HubSpot image block → link the thumbnail to the Sendspark video URL.
- If you prefer the video to play in-line instead, Sendspark also supports embedding it directly in HubSpot emails.

### Image + link vs. in-line embed

| | Image + link (this guide) | Embedded video |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tracking | Track clicks and plays | Less click/play data |
| Consistency | Same preview in every email client | Depends on client support |
| Friction | Slight (recipient clicks through) | None (plays in email) |
| Call-to-action | Add a CTA on the landing page | Limited |

There are 2 ways to send videos in email:

1. With the actual **video embedded in the email**.
2. With a preview **image of the video + a link** to your video.

Sendspark supports both. You can choose which way you to want to use. The main tradeoff to consider is **actual engagement** vs**ability to track engagement**. When you link to the video, you add a little friction, but can gather more data on clicks and plays.

In a world where *if you didn't track it, it didn't happen*, it's a tough choice. I'd recommend testing both opens with an objective goal (like signups, meetings, other conversions), and seeing which way helps you achieve your true objective better.

In this article, we will cover **how to send a video in hubspot as an image + link to a video landing page**. The benefits of sending videos as images are...

- **Better insight** - you can track how viewers are engaging video videos
- **Consistency** - recipients will have the same experience no matter which email client they use, as the preview images will look the same on Gmail, Outlook, etc.
- **Conversions** - you can add a call-to-action on your video landing page to funnel viewers into taking the next step in the customer journey immediately after watching your video.

If you prefer to have the video play in-line, [see how to embed a video in Hubspot emails](/hubspot-static-videos-marketing-hub) here.

## How to send a video through Hubspot as an image

1. Record, request, or upload your video in Sendspark.

![Sendspark dashboard with options to record, request, or upload a video](/images/3377335/f97452d5-image.png)

2. Click "share"

![Sendspark video interface with Share button highlighted in the menu](/images/3377335/24ad0990-image.png)

3. Download your video thumbnail

![Sendspark video thumbnail download option in the sharing settings panel](/images/3377335/9118267d-Screen_Recording_2020-09-03_at_09.28_AM.gif)

4. Make a new email on Hubspot

![HubSpot email editor interface with blank email template ready to edit](/images/3377335/f213e86a-image.png)

5. Upload the video thumbnail to Hubspot in an image block

![HubSpot email editor showing image block upload functionality for video thumbnail](/images/3377335/bbf1a904-Screen_Recording_2020-09-03_at_09.31_AM.gif)

6. Copy the video URL from Sendspark & link it to the video thumbnail in Hubspot

![HubSpot email editor with video thumbnail image and link dialog open](/images/3377335/a5d7eac1-Screen_Recording_2020-09-03_at_09.33_AM.gif)

7. That's it! You're all set to send your video email

![Completed HubSpot email with video thumbnail image linked to video URL](/images/3377335/0a19cfdb-image.png)
