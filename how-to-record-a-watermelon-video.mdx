A "Watermelon" video is a personalized video you record using a dynamic name, where "Watermelon" is the example placeholder name (as in "Hi Watermelon"). For the best AI results, record at least 10 seconds, pause briefly after the name, work in a quiet space close to the microphone, and use Face Mode or Cam Mode so you can add dynamic backgrounds later.

## Key takeaways

- Record for **at least 10 seconds** so the AI has enough voice data to work with.
- **Pause briefly** after saying the dynamic name (for example, "Hi Watermelon").
- Record in a **quiet environment** and stay **close to the microphone**.
- Use **Face Mode or Cam Mode** so you can enable dynamic backgrounds later.

## Best Practices for Recording Personalized Videos

To ensure the best results when generating personalized videos with AI, please follow these recommendations.

---

## 🎯 Pause briefly after the name

If you say a dynamic name (for example: **“Hi Watermelon”**), leave a **short pause after the name** before continuing your sentence.  
This helps the AI process the personalization more naturally.

---

## ⏱️ Record at least 10 seconds

Your recording should be **at least 10 seconds long**.  
Very short recordings may not provide enough voice data for the AI to generate accurate results.

---

## 🔇 Record in a quiet environment

Choose a **silent place** when recording.  
Avoid background noise such as:

- People talking
- Clapping or loud sounds
- Music or TV

---

## 🎙️ Be mindful of microphone sensitivity

Phone microphones can easily pick up **environmental noise**, which can affect the final result.  
Recording in a controlled environment will improve quality.

---

## 🔊 Speak close to the microphone

Make sure you are **close enough to the microphone** and speaking at a clear volume.  
If you are too far away or speaking too softly, the AI output may not sound optimal.

---

## 🎥 Record in Face Mode or Cam Mode

Always record using **Face Mode** or **Cam Mode**.  
This allows you to **enable dynamic backgrounds later** if needed.
