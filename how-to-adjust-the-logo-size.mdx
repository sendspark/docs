This guide explains how to properly adjust your logo size on Sendspark landing pages. If your logo appears too small even when the size setting is at maximum, the issue is likely due to background margins or improper cropping.

## Why Your Logo Appears Small

Even PNG files without visible backgrounds often contain invisible margins that affect how the logo displays. These margins prevent your logo from taking full advantage of the available space, making it appear smaller than expected.

## Step 1: Remove Background (If Needed)

If your logo has a background that you need to remove:

1. Go to **remove.bg** (a free online background removal tool)
2. Upload your logo image
3. Wait for the tool to process and remove the background automatically
4. Download the processed image

**Note:** The downloaded image may appear to have a white background in your file browser, but it actually has a transparent background.

## Step 2: Crop Excess Margins

To remove invisible margins and optimize your logo size:

1. Go to **iloveimg.com** and select "Crop Image"
2. Upload your background-free logo
3. Adjust the crop area to remove excess space around your logo
4. Make sure to crop as close to the logo edges as possible for maximum space utilization
5. Click "Crop Image" to process
6. Download the optimized logo

## Step 3: Upload to Sendspark

1. Navigate to your Sendspark settings
2. Go to **Global Styles**
3. Remove your current logo
4. Upload the newly cropped logo
5. Set the size to maximum if needed
6. Save your changes

## Viewing Your Changes

After uploading your optimized logo:

1. Refresh your landing page to see the updated logo
2. Your logo should now appear significantly larger and properly positioned

**Tip:** Both remove.bg and iloveimg.com are free tools that don't require account registration, making them convenient options for quick logo optimization.

**Note:** Updates to Global Styles may take some time to reflect across all share pages. Our team is working to ensure consistency between landing pages and share pages.
