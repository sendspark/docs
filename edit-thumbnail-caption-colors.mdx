You can fully customize the caption colors on a video thumbnail in Sendspark. Click **Edit Styles** at the bottom of the thumbnail editor, then under **Text Overlay** set the **Text Color** and the **Banner Background Color**. Pick any combination — for example a green banner with white text — or remove the banner entirely and leave the text over your video.

## Key takeaways

- You can customize both the caption's **Text Color** and its **Banner Background Color** on a thumbnail.
- Open these options by clicking **Edit Styles** at the bottom, then looking under **Text Overlay**.
- Mix any colors you like (such as a green banner with white text), or remove the banner and leave the text directly over your video.
- Thumbnail style edits affect only that specific video, not your workspace's global styles.

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I change the caption text and banner colors?">
    Click **Edit Styles** at the bottom of the thumbnail editor. Under **Text Overlay** you'll find the **Text Color** and **Banner Background Color** options, which you can adjust independently.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I remove the banner behind the caption?">
    Yes. You can remove the banner entirely and simply leave the text over your video.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do these color changes affect my other videos?">
    No. Editing your thumbnail styles affects only that specific video. This is different from editing your global styles, which apply to all videos in the workspace.
  </Accordion>
