## 1. Cold Outreach Best Practices

- **Keep videos short and concise** – 30 to 45 seconds is ideal, with a maximum of 1 minute.
  - Skip the “who you are” intro unless it's highly relevant.
- **Thumbnail** - GIFs can help increase engagement so start your video with some movement like an inviting wave to the camera 🤓
- **The second touchpoint is the best place to introduce video** – use video in your second email to help warm up the domain and improve deliverability.
- **Email Copy** - Doesn’t need to be super text-heavy. Make it engaging. You still want to give context, but you can use it as a teaser to spark curiosity and encourage recipients to watch the video.
- **Subject Lines**- create subject lines that are short, personalized, and include the word "video" to boost curiosity and open rates.
  - "Quick video for you, [First Name]"
  - "[First Name], this video is just for you"
  - "Saw this and thought of you – video inside"

## 2. Creating Engaging & Effective Videos

- **Scripts** - Leverage Sendspark’s script generator and teleprompter to craft clear, concise messages.
- **Use 'you' statements** - make your video feel more personalized. Instead of saying *'I wanted to share this*,' try '*This could be really helpful for you because*...' to keep the focus on your prospect.
- **Video Landing Page** - Customize & personalize the page using templates to save time.
  - Templates give you the option to embed your calendar directly onto the landing page, making it easier for your prospect to book a call without interrupting your video.
- **Make eye contact with the camera** - This builds trust and makes the video feel more like a conversation with your prospect

## 3. Choosing the Right Video Style

- **Lip-Sync -** (Works Best with Dynamic Backgrounds)
  - If using lip-sync V-2, pair it with a dynamic background for the best results.
- **Camera-Only (No Dynamic Background) – How to Make It Work:**
  - Try the waving hand trick or mic placement in front of your face to mask any minor lip-sync mismatches. (Waving also makes for an awesome GIF image!)
  - While you don’t need a background, having your prospect’s website behind you can increase engagement by sparking curiosity and driving higher open rates.
- **Using a Virtual Background**
  - You can add a virtual background to create a professional or fun look. (hide your comfy bed in the background 🤪)
- **Combining Videos for Maximum Impact**
  - Whether the first video is face-only or features the prospect’s website as the background, two videos can be combined into one video.
  - The first video can be a personalized introduction, while the second showcases a high-impact visual, such as a dashboard, demo, or key-value prop feature.
  - This approach balances personal connection with valuable insights, making outreach more engaging, effective, and persuasive.
