If your Sendspark video is uploading slowly after recording, the most common cause is a slow internet upload speed. Save your video first, then check your upload speed at Fast.com, restart Chrome, restart your computer, and if it still lags, remove and re-add the Sendspark Chrome extension. A video normally uploads in just a few seconds.

## Key takeaways

- Save your video to your computer first (click the download button in the preview modal) so you don't lose it while troubleshooting.
- Slow uploads are most often caused by a slow internet upload speed — check it at Fast.com before trying anything else.
- Restarting Chrome clears an overloaded browser; restarting your computer clears the whole system if that doesn't help.
- If it still uploads slowly, remove and re-add the Sendspark Chrome extension.

## First: Save your video!

Before you follow any of the steps below, you might want to **save your video to your computer** so you don't lose it.

If you're using the Sendspark Chrome Extension, you can save your video by clicking the "download" button on the left of the preview modal.

![Download button](/images/6226815/3bc6dfe4-image.png)

After saving it to your computer, you can[upload your video to Sendspark](/upload-a-video-to-sendspark) to share it via email or other channels.

## Improve Video Upload Speed

After you record a video using Sendspark, it should upload to Sendspark in just a few seconds. If it feels like your video is taking a long time to upload, you might want to try the following steps:

1. **Check your internet upload speed.** Use Fast to check your internet speed. If your internet speed is slow, then it's normal for your video to upload slowly. There's nothing you can do except sit back and wait patiently.
2. **Restart Chrome.**Sometimes Chrome gets a little overloaded — especially if you have a lot of tabs opened, and haven't restarted your browser recently. Quit Chrome and open it again to freshen up the system.
3. **Restart your computer.**If restarting Chrome doesn't help, try rebooting your entire system.
4. **Remove and re-add the Sendspark Chrome Extension**. If you're still having trouble, try removing and re-adding the [Sendspark Chrome extension](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/sendspark-video-and-scree/blimjkpadkhcpmkeboeknjcmiaogbkph).

![Sendspark Chrome Extension](/images/6226815/4e8f5e75-image.png)

Here's each fix at a glance:

| Step | Fix | When to use it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Check your internet upload speed at Fast.com | Always check first; a slow connection means slow uploads |
| 2 | Quit and reopen Chrome | Browser feels overloaded or has many tabs open |
| 3 | Restart your computer | Restarting Chrome didn't help |
| 4 | Remove and re-add the Sendspark Chrome extension | Uploads are still slow after restarting |
