## 🧠 Guidelines for Creating Effective Dynamic Videos (and How Minute Usage Works)

When creating **dynamic videos** in Sendspark using **AI voice cloning**, it’s important to provide **enough input** for the AI to work with.  
If the video is **too short (under 5 seconds)**, the AI **will likely fail** to generate a natural-sounding voice because there simply isn’t enough voice input.

## 🎯 Why We Recommend a Minimum of 10 Seconds

To ensure the best possible result, we recommend that **dynamic videos be at least 10 seconds long**.  
This gives the AI enough audio to generate a natural-sounding voice and keeps the video from sounding abrupt or “robotic.”

Videos shorter than 10 seconds are prone to quality issues, and videos under 5 seconds often *fail completely*.

## 🧩 Want to Save Minutes? Use Combined Videos

If you’re trying to **save DVM minutes**, here’s the recommended approach:

| Feature | Uses Dynamic Video Minutes | Recommendation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dynamic Video | ✅ Yes | Minimum suggested length: **10 seconds** |
| Combined Video | ❌ No | Use this for the rest of the message |

**Suggested Workflow**

1. Record the **dynamic, personalized** portion of the video (≥ 10 seconds).
2. Add a **combined video** (regular/static video) to extend the total playback time — this **does not** consume DVM minutes.

## FAQ: “If the variable is just a one-second greeting (‘Hey #&#123;&#123;First Name&#125;&#125;’), am I only charged one second?”

No — the **entire length** of the dynamic video counts toward your DVM usage.  
So if your dynamic video is 12 seconds long and the personalized variable is only 1 second of that, **you will still be charged 12 seconds**.
