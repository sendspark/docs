To record a PowerPoint presentation in full screen, hide the slide thumbnails on the left, then open the ribbon drop-down menu and enable "Single Line Ribbon" and "Automatically Hide." This clears the interface so your slides fill the screen while you record. You only need a free Microsoft account, not a paid subscription.

## Key takeaways

- You do **not** need a paid Microsoft subscription — a free Microsoft account with OneDrive access is enough to use this version of PowerPoint.
- **Hide the image thumbnails** on the left side to clear space.
- Open the ribbon drop-down (arrow in the right-hand corner) and enable **"Single Line Ribbon"** and **"Automatically Hide"** to maximize your slide area.
- To record yourself over PowerPoint in full screen, use the [Sendspark Desktop Recorder](/record-over-powerpoint-pdfs-applications) — the Chrome Extension can't record outside the browser.

Note that you do not need an active subscription to Microsoft in order to access PowerPoint. You simply need a free Microsoft account and log into OneDrive to access this PowerPoint version.

1) Start by hiding the image thumbnails on the left:

![PowerPoint - Hide Thumbnail](/images/8444340/24d28129-image.png)

2) Click on the arrow in the right-hand corner to deploy the drop-down menu. Once there, check the "Single Line Ribbon" and the "Automatically Hide"

![PowerPoint - Ribbon Layout and Show Ribbon](/images/8444340/13729ba8-image.png)

3) You are now ready to record!

![PowerPoint - Ready to record](/images/8444340/82d2ba5e-image.png)
