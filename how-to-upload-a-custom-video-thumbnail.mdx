To use a custom video thumbnail in Sendspark, add your own image to your email draft, copy your Sendspark video share link, and hyperlink that image to the share page. Because the video thumbnail and the video landing page are two separate entities, you can link **any** image to your video. When recipients click your custom thumbnail, the full video opens in their browser.

## Key takeaways

- The video **thumbnail** and the video **landing page** are two separate entities, so you can link any image to your Sendspark video.
- Sendspark auto-generates an animated GIF thumbnail, but you can replace it with your own custom image in email.
- The setup is three steps: add your image to the email, copy the video share link, and hyperlink the image to the share page.
- Clicking the custom thumbnail takes recipients to the Sendspark video page to watch in their browser — just like the default GIF.

## Thumbnail options

| Thumbnail | How it's created | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Auto-generated GIF | Sendspark creates an [animated GIF thumbnail](https://www.sendspark.com/video-platform/video-gif-thumbnails) automatically | A quick, motion-based preview with no extra work |
| Custom image | You add your own image and hyperlink it to the video share page | Branded or designed thumbnails you control |

Sendspark automatically generates [animated GIF thumbnails](https://www.sendspark.com/video-platform/video-gif-thumbnails) to embed videos in email — but you don't have to use them!

You can easily add your own custom video thumbnails to your emails.

The important thing to know here is that **the video thumbnail and the video landing page are 2 separate entities.**

This means that you can link **any image** to your Sendspark video landing page!

## How to use a custom video thumbnail in email

<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/DvJVmhE-jb0?rel=0" allow="autoplay; fullscreen; picture-in-picture; encrypted-media" allowFullScreen />

1. Add your custom thumbnail to your email draft (as an image)
2. Find the share link to your video ![Copy Video Share Link](/images/5898638/0d542e9f-image.png)
3. Hyperlink your custom thumbnail to the Sendspark video share page

Now, when recipients receive the email, they will see the custom video thumbnail, and be able to click to view the video in their browser.
